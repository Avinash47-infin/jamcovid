TRAVEL AUTHORISATION FOR JAMAICA

Your application has been approved. You are hereby authorised to travel to Jamaica.

Please ensure you retain either a printed or electronic copy of this authorisation as you will be required to provide it to check in for and board your flight or vessel to Jamaica. You will also be required present your negative COVID-19 PCR or Antigen test certificate to check in and board your flight or vessel. If your travel date is on or before March 10, 2021, your test must have been within 10 days of your date of travel. If your travel date is on or after March 11, 2021, your test must have been within 3 days of your date of travel. The name and date of birth on the test certificate must match those on your passport. Please retain this travel authorisation and your negative COVID-19 test certificate for the duration of your stay in Jamaica.

Note that this travel authorisation allows only the holder of the passport listed below to enter Jamaica. If you intend to travel with other persons, including minors, each person must obtain a separate travel authorisation.

TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF YOUR TRAVEL AUTHORISATION

You are approved to enter Jamaica at this time subject to the following terms and conditions to which you consented as part of your application:

Travel Costs

You acknowledge you are traveling to Jamaica and bear all the costs for travel to and from the destination as well as costs while you are in-country should you fall ill. This can include, but is not limited to the following:

  • COVID-19 test
  • Medical services
  • Hospital services
  • Medical transportation services
  • Medical evacuation services
  • Quarantine/isolation facilities and services

Health Screening and Risk Assessment at Port of Entry

Upon arrival in Jamaica, you will be required to undergo a health screening and risk assessment by the health authorities. The health screening will include checking your temperature, observation of your symptoms, if any, as well as an interview by a health officer.

You understand that you may be subject to testing if you are assessed as high risk either as a result of coming from a country designated as high risk for COVID-19 transmission based on classification by the World Health Organisation or due to other risk factors such as exhibiting symptoms or exposure to persons who have tested positive. You understand that you may still be required to be tested in Jamaica based on a risk assessment by the health authorities even if you were required to present a negative COVID-19 test certificate prior to your arrival.

Testing for COVID-19

You also consent to the following COVID-19 testing protocol, should you be required to be tested by the health authorities:

Naso-pharyngeal and oro-pharyngeal swabbing on my arrival. If your initial test for COVID-19 is positive, or you develop any symptom of COVID-19 during your period of quarantine, you may be placed in mandatory isolation for 14 days or until you have fully recovered. You agree to further samples for testing being taken during your period of isolation.

Stay in COVID-19 Resilient Corridor

You understand that, if you are required to be tested on arrival, you will be required to await your tests results under quarantine in your room at the hotel/resort. If you are not required to be tested or if your test is negative, you understand that you will be required for the duration of your stay in Jamaica to remain in the hotel/resort within the “COVID-19 Resilient Corridor”, a defined geographical area within Jamaica designated for tourism purposes. You may however visit attractions certified by the Jamaica Tourist Board within the “COVID-19 Resilient Corridor” provided that you travel to and from the attractions on transportation licensed under the Tourist Board Act, adhere to the relevant protocols for attractions, and remain with the family or group with which you travelled to the attractions. You must obtain permission from the health authorities to leave the “COVID-19 Resilient Corridor” and, if such permission is granted, you will be required to be in quarantine for a period of 14 days from your date of arrival in Jamaica.

Isolation

If you are required to be tested for COVID-19 and your test is positive, you consent to being isolated either at your hotel/resort or in a government facility as determined by the health authorities.

Governing Laws and Regulations

The above are governed by the following applicable Laws and Regulations of Jamaica:

  • The Constitution of Jamaica
  • The Disaster Risk Management Act
  • The Public Health Act
  • The Tourist Board Act
  • The Aliens Act
  • Immigration Restrictions (Commonwealth Citizens Act)
